## How safe is diving?

Recreational scuba diving, done properly, has a fatality rate of roughly 1 per 200,000 dives — about half the rate of horse riding. The risk is real but it's manageable, and the great majority of incidents are preventable.

Almost every preventable incident has the same shape: a small problem ignored, then compounded by a second small problem, then a third. Good diving is mostly about catching the first one.

## What we do

- **Pre-dive briefings** at every site, every dive, every group. Site, conditions, plan, signals, contingencies.
- **Buddy checks** before every entry. We do them with you, not at you, until you're doing them automatically.
- **Conservative depth and time limits.** Group dives stay well inside no-decompression limits.
- **Direct radio contact** with the boat throughout every dive. Surface support stays at the dive site.
- **Oxygen on board** every boat, refilled regularly, with a current React Right (CPR + first aid) certified crew member.
- **DAN Indonesia partnership** for evacuation support. The closest hyperbaric chamber is in Mataram (Lombok mainland) — 1 hour from us by boat plus ambulance.
- **Annual equipment service** for every regulator, BCD, computer, and tank in our rental fleet.
- **Daily kit checks.** Crew runs through every set every morning.

## What we ask of you

- **Be honest on the medical declaration.** Don't hide conditions because you don't want them to stop you diving — they stop you for a reason.
- **Tell us how you're feeling.** Tired, hungover, light-headed, anxious, "off". We'd rather skip a dive and know than dive and find out at 18 metres.
- **Stay with your buddy.** This is the single rule that protects most divers from most problems.
- **Respect your air, your time, your depth.** Turn pressure means turn pressure. Up means up.
- **Travel insurance with diving cover.** Non-negotiable for any trip with us. Costs a few dollars per day; saves tens of thousands.

## Conditions that pause our diving

We don't dive when:

- Surface conditions exceed 1.2 m swell at any of our sites
- Visibility drops below 5 m for more than two consecutive dives
- Lightning is within 10 km
- Any group member shows symptoms of illness that affect safe diving (sinus, ear, balance, stamina)

We have alternative shallow sites for marginal conditions, and we'd rather refund a day than dive when we shouldn't.

## When things go wrong

We have written incident protocols. The summary:

- Surface, breathe, signal the boat
- Boat to dive site, evacuation kit deployed
- DAN Indonesia called for advice
- Radio to harbour for medical transport
- Speedboat to Mataram if hyperbaric treatment is indicated

In our years operating we have never had a diving-related fatality. We have had a small number of incidents that triggered the protocol above and resolved with no permanent injury — exactly because the protocol is in place.
