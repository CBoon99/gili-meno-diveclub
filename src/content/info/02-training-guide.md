## What we believe about teaching diving

Two principles drive how we run courses:

1. **Comfort comes before skill.** A diver who is comfortable in the water learns faster, is safer, and enjoys themselves. We build comfort first.
2. **Small groups are non-negotiable.** Maximum 4:1 student-to-instructor ratios. We run smaller when we can. Most days you'll be 1:1 or 2:1.

Everything else flows from those two.

## The structure of a course day

A typical Open Water Diver Day 2 looks like:

- **07:30** — Arrive at the shop, coffee, kit assembly with your instructor
- **08:00** — Boat departs harbour
- **08:30** — Briefing on first dive: site, plan, skills, signals
- **09:00-09:50** — First ocean dive
- **10:00** — Surface interval, kit refresh, snacks, debrief
- **10:30** — Briefing on second dive
- **11:00-11:50** — Second ocean dive
- **12:30** — Boat back to harbour, lunch, theory review
- **15:00** — Optional online theory module of the day
- **17:00** — Free time

We deliberately leave room in the schedule. Rushed students don't learn well; tired students aren't safe.

## How we run skills

Skills are introduced **on land** (or in the briefing), **practised in the pool** at a depth of 1-2 m, then **performed at 5-10 m** in the open water. By the time you do a skill on a certification dive, you've done it three or four times in increasingly real conditions.

We also do something most schools don't: we **debrief every skill**. What went well, what was difficult, what you'd change next time. Five minutes of honest debrief saves an hour of repeating the same problem in deeper water.

## Theory

SSI delivers theory online. You can start before you arrive — the material is well-paced and not painful. Most students complete it in the evenings during their course.

We then run **fifteen-minute pool-side theory recaps** before each pool session. Quick, focused, building from what you already know.

## Languages

Our instructors teach in **English** and **French**. We have part-time staff who teach in **Bahasa Indonesia** and **German** — let us know if you'd prefer either when booking and we'll match you if available.

## Crossover students

If you're already certified with PADI, NAUI, CMAS or another agency, we accept your certification as equivalent for course prerequisites. Bring:

- Your certification card (digital is fine)
- Your dive logbook (or an honest verbal account of dive count)

We'll fit you in to the right level of course or fun-dive group. No equivalence fees, no "conversion courses".

## What instructors look for

When we evaluate readiness for certification, we look for:

- **Buoyancy that's good enough.** Not perfect — that takes years.
- **Calm under pressure.** Things go slightly wrong on every dive. We want to see you respond, not panic.
- **Buddy awareness.** Do you check on your buddy without being prompted?
- **Honesty about your limits.** Saying "I'm cold and tired and ready to surface" is a sign of a good diver.

If you're not ready on the timeline of the course, we extend the course. There's no extra charge; the goal is competence, not paperwork.
