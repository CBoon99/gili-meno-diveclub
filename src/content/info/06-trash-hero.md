## Trash Hero in one sentence

Trash Hero is a community movement, started in Thailand in 2013, that runs regular volunteer cleanups in the places its members live and work. The Gili Meno chapter runs a weekly beach cleanup; we add a monthly underwater dive on top.

The model is deliberately simple: show up, pick stuff up, weigh it, log it. No memberships, no fundraising pressure, no hierarchy. Just consistent presence.

## What we do

- **Monthly underwater cleanup dive.** Usually the third Sunday of the month. We take divers to sites where rubbish accumulates (often the lagoon-side reefs after monsoon) and collect what we can.
- **Boat support for beach cleanups.** When Trash Hero needs help moving rubbish from remote beaches, our boat helps.
- **Material support.** Bags, gloves, beach signage — small things that make the operation easier.
- **Educational tie-in.** Our [Eco-Scuba course](/courses/eco-scuba) includes a Trash Hero-coordinated cleanup dive as one of its assessment dives.

## What you can do

- **Join a cleanup dive.** If your stay coincides with the third Sunday, ask us. No charge for divers staying with us during the dive day.
- **Join the beach cleanup.** Weekly, no diving required. Information posted at the Trash Hero noticeboard near the harbour.
- **Reduce your single-use plastic.** Refill bottles at the dive shop (free), refuse straws, choose reef-safe sunscreen, bring your own bag for shopping.
- **Don't tip in plastic-wrapped notes.** Carry small denomination IDR.
- **Donate.** Cash to Trash Hero directly (their tin is at the harbour). We don't take a cut.

## What's actually in the rubbish

For the divers and beach-walkers wondering what we find:

- **Single-use plastic** — bottles, sachets, packaging
- **Fishing gear** — nets, line, hooks. The most dangerous items by a long way; nets entangle turtles, line cuts coral.
- **Cigarette filters** — embedded in beach sand at every popular swimming spot
- **Polystyrene fragments** — from broken float-and-cooler boxes
- **Glass** — bottles, broken glass on the beach

Most of it is land-sourced — runoff from rivers in Bali and Lombok during heavy rain — rather than originating on Gili Meno itself. That's part of why beach cleanup matters: the rubbish is going somewhere, and someone has to take it out of the water.

## What it doesn't do

To be honest about the limits: cleanup dives don't fix the problem. They make a specific reef look better for a week. The actual fix is upstream — better waste management in Bali and Lombok, less single-use plastic in the supply chain, better fishing gear disposal.

We participate because we live here, the reefs are our workplace, and not participating would feel like complicity. We don't claim it's solving anything bigger.

## Get involved

If you'd like to join a cleanup dive, ask at the shop on arrival or message us in advance. Trash Hero schedules a few weeks ahead and we can usually coordinate around it.

[Find Trash Hero on their website](https://trashhero.org/) for the worldwide network.
