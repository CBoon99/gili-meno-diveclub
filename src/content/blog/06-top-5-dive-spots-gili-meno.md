## Why Gili Meno is special

Of the three Gili Islands, Meno is the smallest, the quietest, and arguably the best-positioned for diving. The reefs around it sit in a relatively sheltered passage between Lombok and the deeper Indonesian Throughflow, which means warm water year-round, steady currents that support healthy coral, and a turtle population so reliable we now budget on seeing two or three per fun-dive.

After years of running courses and fun-dives on every site within a 25-minute boat ride of our shop, here are the five we keep going back to.

## 1. Turtle City

If you only do one dive on Gili Meno, this is it. A long sloping garden of *Acropora* and *Porites* corals between 5 m and 18 m, Turtle City is named for one obvious reason: a stable, resident population of green and hawksbill turtles that graze the seagrass and sleep wedged into reef crevices.

- **Depth:** 5 – 18 m
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Best for:** First-time fun-divers, Open Water Diver students, photographers
- **Best months:** May – October (visibility 20 m+)

It also doubles as our main training site for the Open Water and Advanced courses — gentle current, sandy bail-out zones, and a near guarantee of memorable wildlife on the certification dives.

## 2. Meno Wall

A genuine vertical wall that drops from 5 m to past 30 m, Meno Wall is the classic Gili Meno "wow" dive. Sea fans and gorgonians decorate the wall, schools of fusiliers stream past at mid-water, and white-tip reef sharks patrol the deeper end.

- **Depth:** 5 – 30 m
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate (mild current)
- **Best for:** Advanced Adventurer dives, Deep Specialty, photographers
- **Best months:** May – September

You'll typically dive it as a multi-level — drop to 26 m for the wall, work shallower along the top of the slope to clear deco and finish in the coral garden where turtles graze.

## 3. Bounty Wreck

A wholly artificial pontoon wreck deliberately sunk in the early 2000s and now colonised by lionfish, scorpionfish, batfish and the occasional cuttlefish. It sits upright in 18 m of clear sand, with most of the structure within recreational limits.

- **Depth:** 14 – 18 m
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Best for:** Wreck Specialty, Advanced Adventurer, macro photographers
- **Best months:** Year-round

We never penetrate the wreck on recreational dives — Wreck Specialty students do limited overhead-environment work from outside in, with redundant lights and reels, the safe way.

## 4. Shark Point

A series of sandy gullies between low coral ridges, Shark Point lives up to its name with regular sightings of white-tip and (less often) black-tip reef sharks. Stingrays and bamboo sharks rest in the sand; large schools of jacks pass overhead in the wet season.

- **Depth:** 12 – 22 m
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Best for:** Advanced certified divers, fish-ID, drift-diving practice
- **Best months:** April – November

Currents pick up here at slack-tide changes — we dive it on the start of the rising tide for the best mix of clarity and shark activity.

## 5. Sunset Reef (Bio Rock)

A long, gently sloping reef on the western side of Meno, famous for its Bio Rock conservation structures — low-voltage steel frames that accelerate coral regrowth on degraded substrate. The site doubles as a sunset-aligned shore swim and our preferred late-afternoon Eco-Scuba dive.

- **Depth:** 5 – 12 m
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Best for:** Discover Scuba, Eco-Scuba, refresher dives
- **Best months:** Year-round (calmest April – October)

Coral cover here has noticeably increased in the last five years thanks to the Bio Rock project — a quiet success story that's still mostly invisible to people who don't dive.

## Putting them together

A typical 3-day fun-dive package at Meno Dive Club covers at least three of these five — we always start with Turtle City to settle nerves and build buoyancy, save Meno Wall for a peak day with strong visibility, and finish with either Bounty Wreck or Shark Point depending on conditions.

If you've got a week and an Advanced certification, you'll see all five plus another half-dozen sites — Hans Reef on Gili Air, Air Wall, Halik Reef, and the deeper Turbo sites — without ever repeating a dive.
