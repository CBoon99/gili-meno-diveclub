## A different kind of Gili

If you've heard of the Gili Islands, you've probably heard about Gili Trawangan — the largest, busiest, party-island of the three. Gili Meno is the small one in the middle, the one with no scooters, no nightclubs, and a population that's mostly resident families and the people who run the dive shops, warungs, and bungalow lodges.

That makes it a paradise for two kinds of traveller: the ones who came specifically to dive, and the ones who'd had enough of louder islands and wanted somewhere they could actually hear the waves.

## The geography in 90 seconds

- **Where:** West-northwest of Lombok, about 25 minutes by speedboat from Bangsal harbour
- **Size:** Roughly 1.5 km × 1 km, walkable end-to-end in 30 minutes
- **Vibe:** Sand paths, *cidomo* horse-carts, coconut palms, no engines louder than a generator
- **Depths offshore:** Meno sits at the lip of a deep Indonesian channel — diveable reef from 5 m to 30 m within 200 m of the shore

You will not get bored on Gili Meno if you came here to dive. You may get bored if you came here to club. (Trawangan is a 10-minute *kapal* across.)

## Why divers love it

**Healthy reefs, almost everywhere.** Meno sits inside a marine protected area that includes most of the western and northern reefs. The result: living *Acropora* gardens that you can touch (with your eyes only) within five minutes of leaving the harbour.

**Turtles, reliably.** We don't promise turtles — that's a tourism-board cliché — but we have a stable population of greens and hawksbills that we encounter on at least 90% of fun-dives. See [our top 5 dive spots](/blog/top-5-dive-spots-gili-meno/) for where to find them.

**Easy logistics.** Every dive site is within 5–25 minutes by boat. No long crossings, no pre-dawn starts, no extended surface intervals on a swelly anchor. You dive twice in the morning, eat lunch at the shop, and you're done by 3 pm with the rest of the day free.

**No motor-boat traffic.** The Gilis ban motorbikes and cars. The combination of low surface noise and a shallow reef-flat makes the island uniquely good for free-diving and snorkelling between dives.

## What else there is to do

Gili Meno is a small island, but it punches above its weight for non-diving days:

- **Snorkelling at Meno Slope** — turtles in 4 m of water from a beach entry
- **Salt-lake walk** — a brackish lake in the centre of the island, mangrove fringes, herons and kingfishers
- **Sunset at Bio Rock beach** — see the conservation structures from the surface, and watch the sun drop behind Bali
- **Yoga on the beach** — a half-dozen low-key shalas
- **Boat day-trip to Trawangan or Air** — for nightlife or coffee culture, then come home to the quiet

## Getting there

- **From Bali:** Fast-boat from Padangbai or Serangan to Gili Meno (4–5 hours total, ~IDR 500k)
- **From Lombok International Airport (LOP):** Taxi to Bangsal Harbour (90 min, IDR 350k) + shuttle boat to Meno (25 min, IDR 35k public, IDR 200k private)
- **From Mataram / Senggigi:** Land transfer + Bangsal boat

The shuttle-boat between the three Gilis runs three times a day in both directions; if you've based on Trawangan or Air and want to dive Meno's reefs, that's an option too.

## Where to stay

We work with several family-run guesthouses on the island for our [Dive & Stay packages](/info/dive-and-stay/) — from beach-side bungalows to slightly fancier resorts. Walk-ins almost always find a bed in the off-season; book ahead in July, August, and around Christmas.

## Why come now

Gili Meno is changing slowly — it always does — but the combination of marine protection, a small permanent population, and a community of operators who care about the reef means this is genuinely one of Indonesia's best small islands for diving in 2026. Visit before everyone else figures it out.
