# When to Dive Gili Meno

Gili Meno offers great diving year-round, but conditions vary dramatically by season. Here's your complete guide to planning the perfect diving trip.

## Dry Season (April - October) ⛅

**Best Overall**: May - September

**Conditions:**
- Calm seas and excellent visibility (15-25m+)
- Minimal rain
- Stable temperatures (27-28°C)
- Moderate currents on exposed sites

**What You'll See:**
- Peak turtle season (especially May-July)
- Pelagic fish actively hunting
- Healthy coral conditions
- Optimal underwater photography light

**Downsides:**
- Busier (book courses in advance)
- Higher prices

**Best For:** Experienced divers seeking challenging sites, photographers, turtle encounters

## Wet/Monsoon Season (November - March) 🌧️

**Quietest Period**: November - January

**Conditions:**
- Variable visibility (8-15m)
- Rain and choppy seas (especially afternoons)
- Warmer water (28-29°C)
- Fewer boats, fewer crowds

**What You'll See:**
- Fewer tourists = more wildlife comfort
- Nocturnal creatures active
- Different fish behavior
- Macro photography opportunities

**Best For:** Budget travelers, macro photographers, those wanting a quieter experience

## Shoulder Seasons (November, April) 🌤️

Unpredictable but often pleasant. Good compromise between price and conditions.

## What to Pack Year-Round

- **3mm Wetsuit** (always, even though water is warm—sun protection!)
- **50+ SPF Reef-Safe Sunscreen**
- **Sun Protection Shirt**
- **Light Rain Jacket** (for boat travel)
- **Seasickness Medication** (optional, but helpful in monsoon)

## Book in Advance?

**Dry Season (May-Sept):** Book 4-6 weeks ahead. Courses fill quickly.

**Wet Season (Nov-Mar):** Book 1-2 weeks ahead. Much more availability.

## Pro Tips

- **Shoulder Seasons Are Underrated**: Less crowded, decent visibility, special whale/manta encounters
- **Dive Multiple Seasons**: Conditions change what you'll see
