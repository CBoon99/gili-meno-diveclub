## Why SSI?

SSI — Scuba Schools International — is one of the world's two largest dive training agencies (the other being PADI). We chose SSI when we opened the dive shop in 2018 because:

- **Student-centred standards.** SSI's standards put performance over rote-counting; you progress when you can actually do the skill, not when you've ticked a box.
- **Free digital learning.** All theory is delivered through the SSI app, included in your course price. No separate book purchase, no printed manual you'll never read again.
- **Internationally recognised.** SSI is a full WRSTC member alongside PADI, NAUI, BSAC, and the others. Your card is accepted everywhere in the world that accepts diving certifications.
- **Cross-certifications welcome.** Already PADI Open Water? You can carry on with SSI Advanced (or any other SSI course) without re-doing your beginner cert. We do this every week.

## What your first dive looks like

If you've never breathed compressed air underwater, the first day on a course is the most important thing we'll do together. It happens in the morning, in our training pool (chest-deep, calm, warm) with one instructor and a maximum of four students. You'll:

1. Watch a 30-minute SSI digital theory module on the basics of pressure, breathing, and underwater communication.
2. Get fitted for kit — mask, fins, BCD, regulator, computer.
3. Spend 90 minutes in the pool practising the four core skills: breathing through the regulator, clearing water from a mask, recovering a regulator if it falls out, and basic buoyancy.
4. Lunch.
5. Optional first ocean dive at our shore site (8 m, 50 m offshore, soft sand).

Most students leave the first day excited and a little tired. That's normal. Day 2 is more pool, more skills; Day 3 is two ocean dives at Turtle City; Day 4 is two ocean dives at a second site for the certification.

## Course menu

We run the full SSI ladder of recreational diving:

- **[Try Scuba / Discover Scuba](/courses/specialty-courses/)** — pool-only or pool + 1 ocean dive, no certification, IDR 1,200,000 / day
- **[Open Water Diver](/courses/open-water/)** — 3-4 days, full certification to 18 m
- **[Advanced Adventurer](/courses/advanced-open-water/)** — 2 days, certification to 30 m + 5 specialty samplers
- **[Stress & Rescue](/courses/rescue-diver/)** — 3-4 days, the most useful course you'll ever take
- **[Divemaster](/courses/divemaster/)** — 14+ days, professional rating, internships available
- **[SSI Specialty Courses](/courses/specialty-courses/)** — Deep, Night, Wreck, Photo, Buoyancy, Drift… 1-2 days each

## What if I'm only here for a week?

Plenty of time for Open Water Diver (3-4 days) followed by 2-4 fun-dives, or for Advanced Adventurer if you're already certified. A week with us typically looks like:

- Day 1: Arrive, pool session, online theory
- Days 2-4: Confined + open-water training, certification
- Days 5-6: Fun-dives on Meno Wall, Bounty Wreck, or Shark Point
- Day 7: Travel out (we deliberately don't dive your last day so you fly safely)

## Junior Open Water (ages 10-14)

We're licensed to teach Junior Open Water Diver from age 10. Junior students dive with a parent or instructor only, to a depth limit of 12 m up to age 12 and 18 m up to age 14. It's the same syllabus and the same certification, with that adjusted depth limit until they age into full Open Water at 15.
