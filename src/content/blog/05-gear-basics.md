# To Buy or Rent: A Diver's Guide

One of the biggest questions new divers ask: should I invest in my own gear? The answer depends on how often you'll dive and how committed you are to the hobby.

## The Rental Option

**When You're Starting Out**  
Rent for your first 20-30 dives while you figure out what you actually like and what fits your body.

**Why Rent:**
- No upfront investment (courses + rental = $200-400)
- No maintenance hassle
- Easy to try different setups
- Less to travel with

**Downsides:**
- Can feel uncomfortable (doesn't fit perfectly)
- Quality varies
- Costs add up ($30-50 per dive × 20 dives = $600-1000)

## The Investment

**When You're Serious**  
After 20-30 dives, buying personal gear makes financial and practical sense.

**Essential Gear to Own:**

**Mask & Fins** ($150-250)
- Most personal item (fit matters!)
- Easy to travel with
- Immediate ROI: stops rental costs

**Wetsuit** ($100-300)
- Personal fit essential
- Rental suits are often cold/uncomfortable
- 3mm is standard for tropics

**Dive Computer** ($200-800)
- Safety investment
- Tracks all your dives
- Personal preference (wrist vs console)

**What to Rent:**
- Tank (heavy, bulky)
- BCD (varies by body type)
- Regulator (unless you dive 50+ times/year)
- Lights for night dives

## Budget Breakdown

**Minimal Setup (Starting):**
- Certification: $350
- Rental per dive: $40

**Personal Gear (After 30 Dives):**
- Mask & Fins: $200
- Wetsuit: $150
- Dive Computer: $300
- **Total: $650** (but pays for itself in ~13-15 dives)

**Full Setup (When Serious):**
- All above + BCD ($300-500) + Regulator ($400-700)
- **Total: $1500-2000**

## Maintenance Tips

If you do buy:
- Rinse all gear in fresh water after dives
- Dry completely before storing
- Check seals and zippers regularly
- Have regulators serviced annually

## Quality vs Price

**Budget Brands** ($100-200)  
Fine for casual divers, adequate quality

**Mid-Range** ($200-500)  
Best value, reliable brands (DiveRite, TUSA, Mares)

**Premium** ($500+)  
Technical features, durability, but overkill for recreational diving

## Our Recommendation

**Start Renting** for your first 20 dives. Then invest in mask/fins and a dive computer. You'll know whether diving is a lifelong passion or just a vacation activity.
