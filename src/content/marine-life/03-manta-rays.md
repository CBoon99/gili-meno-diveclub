## Honest about what you can expect

Manta rays are **not resident around Gili Meno**. We're going to be honest about that up front because plenty of dive shops are not.

The closest reliable manta cleaning station to us is at **Manta Point** off Nusa Penida (Bali), about 4 hours away by fast-boat plus dive boat. The most reliable manta diving in eastern Indonesia is at **Komodo National Park**, ~1.5 hours by plane from Bali.

What we *do* see locally are **eagle rays**, occasionally drifting past the deeper sites in the open water beyond the reef edges, and occasional sightings of mantas during their seasonal movement through the Lombok Strait.

## When mantas pass through

Reef mantas can appear off Gili Meno during the **wet-to-dry transition months (February-April)**, when plankton blooms following the rains attract them inshore. These sightings are unpredictable — typically one to three per month across the whole dive cluster, not per day.

If you're prioritising mantas, **plan a Komodo or Nusa Penida side trip rather than rely on Gili Meno**. We're happy to recommend operators we trust for both.

## Eagle rays — what we do see

**Spotted eagle rays (Aetobatus narinari)** are a more reliable encounter at Gili Meno. They tend to cruise in the open water between coral mounds at:

- **[Deep Turbo](/dive-sites/deep-turbo)** — most common eagle-ray site
- **[Meno Wall](/dive-sites/meno-wall)** — occasional sightings along the wall
- **[Secret Reef](/dive-sites/secret-reef)** — small numbers, deeper sections

Eagle rays are spectacular animals — wingspans of 1.5-3 m, distinctive spotted pattern, gliding flight — and seeing them gracefully pass overhead is one of the underrated highlights of a Meno dive.

## Etiquette around any large ray

- **Stay low and still.** Mantas and eagle rays will sometimes approach if you don't pursue them.
- **Never touch.** Their slime coating is critical to their immune system.
- **No flash on close-range shots.** Disorienting for them and useless for your photo.
- **Don't block their path.** They need open water to manoeuvre.
