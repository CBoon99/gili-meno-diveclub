## Beyond the headline animals

Turtles, sharks, and rays make the brochures. But the reason serious divers come back to Gili Meno is the small stuff — the everyday fish life and macro creatures that make every dive feel like a slow exploration of someone else's neighbourhood.

## What you'll see on every dive

The "always there" cast:

- **Parrotfish** — bumphead, blue-spot, daisy, and a dozen other species. Listen for the crunch of their beaks on coral.
- **Wrasses** — humphead (rare), cleaner wrasses (everywhere, working at cleaning stations), and a parade of smaller species
- **Anthias** — the orange-and-yellow clouds hovering above coral heads, especially early morning
- **Snappers and fusiliers** — schooling fish that cruise past in tight ribbons
- **Groupers** — usually solitary, sometimes massive, watching from beneath ledges

## The macro life that rewards slowness

Many of our most experienced guests deliberately dive **slow and shallow** to find the small things:

- **Nudibranchs** — sea slugs in colours that shouldn't exist in nature. Bio Rock and Shallow Turbo have particularly diverse populations.
- **Octopuses** — common at dawn and dusk, especially at Sunset Reef and Halik Reef
- **Mantis shrimps** — peering out of holes in the rubble
- **Frogfish** — occasionally on Secret Reef and Meno Wall sponges
- **Ghost pipefish** — seasonal, in seagrass and around gorgonians
- **Banded coral shrimps and dancing shrimps** — at every cleaning station once you know what to look for

## Best sites for macro

- **[Bio Rock](/dive-sites/bio-rock)** — artificial reef structures densely colonised, easy to find macro life
- **[Shallow Turbo](/dive-sites/shallow-turbo)** — slow, shallow, photographer-friendly
- **[Halik Reef](/dive-sites/halik-reef)** — calm and shallow, lots of small life
- **[Secret Reef](/dive-sites/secret-reef)** — deeper macro on the gorgonians and sponges

## How to find more of it

The single biggest improvement to your underwater fish-watching is **slowing down**. New divers cover too much ground; experienced divers hover.

Our [SSI Specialty courses](/courses/specialty-courses) include **Fish ID**, **Marine Ecology**, and **Underwater Photography** — any of which will roughly double the number of species you notice on every subsequent dive.

[Book a fun-dive day](/booking) and ask for a "slow dive" — we'll match you with a guide who'd rather hover for ten minutes over a single coral head than rush past everything.
