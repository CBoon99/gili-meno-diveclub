## Dive with purpose

Most diving courses teach you how to be safe in the water. This one teaches you how to be useful to it.

We built the Eco-Scuba programme around what we actually do every week on Gili Meno: monitoring our local reefs, recording the changes we see, joining Trash Hero cleanup dives, and helping new divers behave around coral and turtles in a way that doesn't damage them.

### What you'll learn

- How a coral reef works as an ecosystem — and what threatens it here in the Gilis
- Coral identification: the species you'll meet on every dive at Meno
- Fish identification: the families and the indicator species reef scientists watch
- How to behave around turtles, reef sharks, octopuses, and macro life — without stress to them
- Buoyancy and finning techniques for diving over delicate substrates
- How to record what you see in a way that's useful to local conservation teams

### Course structure

- **Day 1** — Theory in the morning, then a long, slow reef survey dive in the afternoon at Bio Rock or Halik Reef
- **Day 2** — A community cleanup dive coordinated with Trash Hero Gili Meno, then a second dive recording what we found and didn't find

### What's included

- The relevant SSI ecology specialty (most students take Marine Ecology or Coral Identification)
- Online theory and digital certification
- All equipment, tanks, weights, boat transfers
- A donation per student goes directly to Trash Hero Gili Meno's running costs

### After Eco-Scuba

We invite Eco-Scuba graduates to join our monthly community cleanup dives free of air fills and equipment for the rest of their stay. Several have stayed on as volunteer divemasters during their gap year — get in touch if that's of interest.
