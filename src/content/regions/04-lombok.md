## Lombok in a nutshell

Lombok is the larger neighbour of Bali — quieter, more rural, and home to the Rinjani volcano in the centre. For divers, Lombok is mostly the gateway to the Gili Islands rather than a dive destination in its own right.

The big dive sites of the Lombok Strait — Meno Wall, Hans Reef, Turtle City — are technically off Lombok administratively, but the diving is run from the Gili Islands rather than the Lombok mainland.

## How divers use Lombok

- **Fly in to Lombok International Airport** (LOP), then drive 1.5 hours north to Bangsal harbour
- **Catch a public boat** from Bangsal to Gili Meno (15 minutes), Gili Air (15 minutes), or Gili Trawangan (25 minutes)
- **Many divers stay one night** in Senggigi or Mataram before continuing to the Gilis

## Lombok mainland diving

The Lombok mainland has a few dive sites of its own — Sekotong on the south-west coast and the Belongas Bay area — but these are more remote, harder to reach, and run by different operators.

We focus exclusively on the Gili Islands' shared coral plateau, which is the strongest, most consistent diving in the Lombok Strait.

## Onward to the Gilis

When you're ready to dive, you're heading north. The closest Gili to dive with us is [Gili Meno](/regions/gili-meno) — pretty quiet, very turtle-rich. The biggest is [Gili Trawangan](/regions/gili-trawangan) for accommodation choice; the friendliest middle is [Gili Air](/regions/gili-air).
