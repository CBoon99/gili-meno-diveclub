## A long way east

Komodo National Park is a 1.5-hour flight east of Bali, in eastern Nusa Tenggara. The diving is among the best in the world — manta rays at Manta Point, dramatic current dives at Castle Rock and Crystal Rock, and the seamount sites of the south.

We don't run trips to Komodo. The diving there is current-heavy, depth-heavy, and almost exclusively done from liveaboards (multi-day boat trips) rather than day-trip dive shops.

## Why pair Komodo with Gili Meno

- Komodo demands intermediate-to-advanced skills. Build them in the calmer water of Gili Meno first.
- Liveaboards are exhausting. A few days of relaxed diving from Meno before or after a Komodo trip works well.
- Komodo is expensive. Mix in a week of Gili Meno course-and-fun-dives to balance the budget.

## Pre-Komodo at Gili Meno

If you're booked on a Komodo liveaboard and not yet certified beyond Open Water, we'd suggest:

- **[SSI Advanced Adventurer](/courses/advanced-open-water)** — five adventure dives across two days. Komodo guides will assume you have at least this level.
- **Drift specialty** (within Advanced Adventurer's drift adventure dive) — Meno Wall and Air Wall give you genuine drift experience.
- **A few fun dives** at increasing depths to build comfort below 18 m.

## Post-Komodo at Gili Meno

Coming off a Komodo liveaboard with new skills and tired lungs? A few relaxed days at Hans Reef and Turtle City before flying home are exactly the right energy level.

We can also run **[Stress & Rescue](/courses/rescue-diver)** for divers who realised on their liveaboard that they'd like to be the calmer person on the boat next time.

## Getting to Komodo

- Fly Denpasar (Bali) → Labuan Bajo (Flores). 1.5 hours, 4-6 daily.
- From Labuan Bajo, liveaboards depart for 3-7 night trips into the park.
- Most liveaboards book out 6-12 months ahead in peak season.

We can introduce you to Komodo operators we trust if you're planning the combo trip.
