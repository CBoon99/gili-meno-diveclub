## The classic side trip

The most common itinerary in our booking inbox: a week or two in Bali, with a five-day side trip to the Gilis to learn or progress diving. It's the right call — Bali's diving is fine but crowded, and the Gilis offer better instruction-to-student ratios and easier conditions.

## Getting from Bali to Gili Meno

Three departure points on Bali run fast-boats to the Gilis:

- **Padangbai** (east coast) — most operators, 2 hours direct
- **Serangan** (south, near Denpasar) — 2.5 hours
- **Sanur** — 2.5-3 hours

All three drop at Gili Trawangan, then continue to Gili Air and Gili Meno. Most days have departures at 8 AM and 1:30 PM, with smaller operators in between.

**Cost** — IDR 350,000 - 700,000 one-way (~USD 22 - 45) depending on operator and season.

**Booking** — book the day before via your accommodation or Klook. We don't sell boat tickets, but we can recommend reliable operators.

## Itinerary suggestion

A five-day Open Water side trip from Bali might look like:

- **Day 1** — Morning fast-boat from Bali, arrive Gili Meno by lunch, settle in
- **Days 2-4** — Three days of Open Water Diver course
- **Day 5** — Morning fun dive to celebrate certification, afternoon fast-boat back to Bali

## Which Bali → Gili Meno?

If your hotel is in Ubud, Sanur, or Seminyak, **Padangbai** is the closest fast-boat departure and usually the cheapest. If you're staying further south (Uluwatu, Jimbaran), **Serangan** is closer.

We can advise based on where you're staying — just include your Bali accommodation in your booking message.

## Diving Bali itself

Bali has its own famous dive sites — Tulamben (USS Liberty wreck), Nusa Penida (mola mola), Menjangan in the north-west — but these are run by Bali-based operators. We're focused exclusively on the Gilis.
