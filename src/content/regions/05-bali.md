## The classic side trip

The most common itinerary in our inbox: **one to two weeks in Bali**, then **four to seven nights on the Gili Islands** to learn or progress diving. It works well because Bali handles international flights and culture; the Gilis deliver **calmer student conditions**, **short boat hops to reefs**, and **small-group SSI courses** than many crowded Bali beach resorts.

If you are flying from **Australia**, read our dedicated [**Australia → Indonesia diving hub**](/regions/australia-indonesia-diving) for visas, insurance, and season timing before you lock boat tickets.

## Getting from Bali to Gili Meno

Three main departure areas on Bali run **fast boats** to the Gilis:

| Harbour | Best if you are staying in… | Time to Gilis (typical) |
|---------|------------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Padangbai** | Ubud, Candidasa, Sanur, Seminyak (via east coast) | ~2 h direct |
| **Serangan** | Denpasar, Jimbaran, Nusa Dua | ~2.5 h |
| **Sanur** | Sanur beach strip | ~2.5–3 h |

Boats almost always call at **Gili Trawangan** first, then **Gili Air**, then **Gili Meno**. Morning departures (~**08:00**) and early afternoon (~**13:30**) are standard; always confirm the day before — swell can delay the smallest operators.

**Cost** — roughly **IDR 350,000–700,000** one-way (~**AUD 35–70** at typical rates), depending on operator and season.

**Booking** — through your hotel, reputable ticket offices, or apps like Klook. We do not sell boat tickets, but we can point you to operators that have good safety records and clear cancellation policies.

## Luggage, fins, and domestic connections

**If you are bringing your own BCD or regs** — check your airline’s sports-equipment allowance; many Australian and SE Asian carriers include a **23 kg** bag but charge extra for second pieces. **Fins** often fit inside a checked bag; **mask** goes in carry-on.

**Connecting via Jakarta (CGK) or Surabaya** — allow **3 h minimum** between international and domestic legs; Indonesian domestic flights can move gates quickly.

## Itinerary suggestion: Open Water from Bali

A **five-day** Open Water side trip from Bali might look like:

- **Day 1** — Morning fast-boat from Bali → Gili Meno by lunch → check in → meet the shop, try masks if needed
- **Days 2–4** — **SSI Open Water Diver** pool + ocean schedule (we never rush; extra half-day is free if you need it)
- **Day 5** — Optional **celebration fun dive** at [Turtle City](/dive-sites/turtle-city) or [Meno Wall](/dive-sites/meno-wall), afternoon boat back to Bali

Tighter schedule? Start theory on the **SSI app** before you leave home — saves a full day on island.

## Which harbour should you use?

- **Ubud / central Bali** → **Padangbai** is usually closest and cheapest.
- **Uluwatu / Bukit** → **Serangan** saves an hour in traffic.
- **Nusa Penida day-trip divers** — you are already east; Padangbai is the natural jump.

## Diving Bali itself vs the Gilis

Bali has world-famous sites — **Tulamben** (USS Liberty), **Nusa Penida** (manta and seasonal mola mola), **Menjangan** in the north-west — run by **Bali-based operators**. We are **based on Gili Meno** and focus on the **thirteen sites** of the Lombok Strait plateau: turtles, walls, wrecks, and drift dives without the Denpasar traffic.

Many guests do **two or three days diving Bali**, then **four nights with us** for certification — the best of both worlds.

## South-East Asia hub travellers

If you are already in **Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, or Bangkok**, cheap flights to **DPS** or **LOP** are easy to find. The Gilis are then one fast-boat or one domestic hop away — same advice as above for boats and [Lombok transfers](/regions/lombok).
