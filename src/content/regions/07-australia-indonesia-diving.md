## Why Australians pick the Gili Islands

Indonesia is the closest world-class tropical diving to much of Australia. The **Gili Islands** (Gili Meno, Gili Air, Gili Trawangan) sit in the **Lombok Strait** — warm water year-round, turtles on almost every dive at the right sites, and **SSI** and **PADI** cross-cert courses run in small groups. **Gili Meno** is the quietest of the three: no scooters, minimal nightlife, and a family-operated dive scene that suits divers who want reefs over parties.

If you are comparing **Bali** vs **Gilis** for learning to dive: Bali has famous sites (Liberty wreck, Nusa Penida), but the Gilis typically offer **calmer student conditions**, **shorter boat rides**, and **4:1** instructor ratios. Many Australian travellers do **one week in Bali** plus **four to six nights on Gili Meno** for an Open Water or Advanced course — see our [Bali → Gili Meno guide](/regions/bali).

## Flights: east coast vs Perth

**East coast (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane)** — direct services to **Denpasar (DPS)** are common (roughly **6–7 hours**). From there you either stay in Bali and take a **fast boat** to the Gilis, or connect to **Lombok (LOP)** and transfer north to **Bangsal** harbour for a short public boat to Meno.

**Perth** — much shorter hop to Indonesia (**~3 hours** to Bali). Same onward pattern: fast boat from Padangbai / Serangan / Sanur, or fly to Lombok.

**Typical total journey time** (door to Gili Meno): **12–18 hours** from Australia depending on connections — worth booking an **extra buffer day** before your course starts so jet lag and missed boats do not cost you pool time.

## Visas and entry (check before travel)

Indonesia visa rules change; always confirm on the official **eVOA** / embassy site before you book. Most Australian tourists use a **visa on arrival** or **eVOA** for short leisure stays. Keep **six months validity** on your passport and a **return or onward ticket** — immigration sometimes asks.

## Dive and travel insurance (non-negotiable)

Your Australian domestic health cover **does not** cover hyperbaric treatment overseas. Buy a policy that explicitly lists **recreational scuba diving** to your planned depth, **medical evacuation**, and **trip cancellation**. **DAN** (Divers Alert Network) is widely used by Australian divers as a supplement; combine it with standard travel insurance if required by your course operator.

## Seasons — how Indonesian weather lines up with Australia

**Dry season (roughly May–October)** — best visibility (often **15–25 m** on Gili Meno), calmer seas, peak tourism. Book courses a few weeks ahead in July–August.

**Wet / monsoon (roughly November–April)** — still diveable; visibility can drop (**~10–15 m**), afternoon chop is more common. Fewer crowds and often better accommodation prices.

Water temperature is typically **27–29 °C** — a **3 mm wetsuit** is standard; some Australians skip the full suit in the warmest months and use a shorty plus rash vest.

## Money and phones

**IDR** is the only practical currency on the Gilis; carry cash or use ATMs on Trawangan if you need top-ups. **AUD** is not accepted at most small businesses.

**Mobile**: Australian carriers charge roaming; a local **eSIM** or prepaid SIM bought at DPS or LOP is cheaper for a two-week trip. Download offline maps for Gili Meno — paths are sand and addresses are informal.
