## Gili Meno in one paragraph

Gili Meno is the smallest and quietest of the three Gili Islands, a flat, car-free coral cay sitting between Bali and Lombok. The island is roughly 2 km long, 1 km wide, and walkable end-to-end in 90 minutes. There are no scooters and no traffic. There are turtles in the shallows you can reach from the beach.

The diving is some of the most consistently good in Indonesia: shallow protected reefs on the inside, dramatic walls and drop-offs on the outside, and a current-fed channel between Meno and its neighbours that pulls in trevally, mackerel, and reef sharks.

## Why divers choose Gili Meno

- Thirteen named dive sites within a 15-minute boat ride
- Sea turtles on virtually every dive at the right sites
- Sites for every level — Halik Reef and Hans Reef for beginners, Meno Wall and Deep Turbo for advanced
- A small, quiet island for the time between dives
- Active conservation community: Trash Hero cleanups, the Bask underwater statues, the local turtle sanctuary

## Best months to dive Gili Meno

| Season | Months | Visibility | Conditions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dry / peak | May - October | 15-25 m | Calm seas, light current, busiest |
| Wet / quiet | November - March | 10-15 m | More rain, choppier afternoons, fewer divers |
| Shoulder | April, November | 12-20 m | Variable but often excellent |

Water temperature stays between 27-29 °C year-round, so a 3 mm wetsuit (or even a rash-vest plus shorts) is plenty.

## Getting to Gili Meno

The most common routes:

- **From Bali** — public fast-boats from Padangbai or Serangan harbours. Two to three hours depending on conditions.
- **From Lombok mainland (Bangsal harbour)** — public boats and shuttles. Twenty to thirty minutes.
- **From Gili Trawangan or Gili Air** — five-minute local "boat-shuttle" between the three islands.

## Beyond diving

- **Snorkel** at Turtle City and the Bask Underwater Statues
- **Walk** the loop of the island in 90 minutes
- **Visit the Turtle Sanctuary** in the centre of the island
- **Eat** at the warungs along the harbour-side or one of the beachfront restaurants
- **Sunset** on the west side — there's a reason it's called Sunset Reef
