## Secret Reef

Secret Reef sits east of Gili Meno and lives up to its name — most boats run past it without stopping. The site is a layered coral garden with dramatic overhangs, small caves, and ridges that drop from 10 m down to 35 m. The structure attracts pelagics: schooling trevally, sweetlips, and the occasional reef shark patrolling the deeper edges.

Macro divers will find Secret Reef equally rewarding. Spanish dancers in the overhangs, ornate ghost pipefish in the gorgonians, and pygmy seahorses on the deeper sea fans if you know what you're looking for.

**Marine life** — trevally schools, groupers, snappers, turtles, white-tip and black-tip reef sharks, octopuses, Spanish dancers, ghost pipefish.

**Conditions** — moderate to strong current at depth, visibility 12-18 m, watch your buoyancy near the overhangs.

**Best months** — May to September.

**Depth** — 10-35 m. The shallows are accessible to Open Water divers; the deeper sections need Advanced.

**Photography** — both macro and wide-angle work. Bring a slate so you can point things out without disturbing them.

**Safety** — easy to drift past on a strong current. Stay close to your guide and keep watch on your air. The overhangs are tempting but easy to wedge into.
