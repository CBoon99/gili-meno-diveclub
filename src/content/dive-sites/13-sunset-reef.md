## Sunset Reef

Sunset Reef is a wide, shallow reef on the west side of Gili Meno that's at its absolute best in the late afternoon. As the sun drops, the slanted light comes through the water column at an angle that turns ordinary fish silver and ordinary corals copper.

It's also where you can dive the **dusk window** — that 20 minutes around sunset when daytime fish bed down and nocturnal hunters come out. You don't need a Night Diving certification for it because we're back on the boat before full dark; but you get a glimpse of the night reef without the full commitment.

**Marine life** — parrotfish, snappers, turtles, reef cuttlefish at dusk, mantis shrimps, octopuses emerging at sunset, the first bioluminescent plankton appearing as the light fails.

**Conditions** — calm, shallow, excellent visibility (10-18 m).

**Best months** — year-round; afternoons in the dry season are particularly magical.

**Depth** — 5-20 m.

**Perfect for** — Open Water divers, photographers chasing golden-hour underwater light, anyone curious about night diving but not yet certified.

**Tip** — bring a torch even for a sunset dive. Things get dark in the last 10 minutes.
