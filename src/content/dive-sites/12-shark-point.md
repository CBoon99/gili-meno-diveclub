## Shark Point

Shark Point is the most reliable site on Gili Meno for reef shark encounters. The topography is a mix of sandy channels and coral outcrops at 20-28 m, with a series of overhangs where white-tip reef sharks rest during the day. Black-tips patrol the deeper sand. Eagle rays, jacks, and trevally schools cruise through.

The sharks here are habituated to divers but not aggressive — they do exactly what reef sharks do, which is mind their own business and occasionally check you out.

**Marine life** — white-tip and black-tip reef sharks, jacks, trevally, schooling fusiliers, eagle rays, turtles, octopuses in the rubble.

**Conditions** — moderate current at depth, visibility 14-20 m.

**Best months** — May to October.

**Depth** — 12-28 m.

**Suitable for** — Advanced Adventurer divers and above with comfortable buoyancy. Not a beginner site.

**Photography** — wide-angle, low ISO, fast shutter. Sharks are usually moving; freeze them.

**Etiquette** — do not chase, do not touch, do not block their path. Stay low and let them come to you.
