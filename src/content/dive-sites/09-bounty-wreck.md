## Bounty Wreck

The Bounty is a small wooden vessel sunk deliberately some years back as an artificial reef. She sits upright on a sandy bottom at around 18 m at the deck and 22 m at the keel, fully encrusted in hard and soft coral, and now serves as home to grouper, snapper, jackfish, and a resident moray that usually sleeps under the bow.

The wreck is small enough to swim around in 10 minutes and big enough to spend an hour exploring slowly. Photographers love it for the silhouettes; macro divers love it for the shrimps and crabs that have colonised every joint.

**Marine life** — groupers, snappers, jackfish, turtles cruising past, moray eels, lionfish, banded coral shrimps, occasional squid in the shallows on the way up.

**Conditions** — moderate current, visibility 15-20 m.

**Best months** — May to October.

**Depth** — 15-35 m around the wreck and surrounding sand.

**Suitable for** — Advanced Adventurer divers and above. We do not penetrate the wreck on recreational dives.

**Photography** — wide-angle silhouette shots from below the wreck, macro of the encrusting life, behaviour shots of the resident moray.

**Note** — fishing line and old netting still occasionally drift onto the wreck. Stay outside, mind your gauges and SPG hose around protruding metal, and let your guide handle any cleanup.
