## Turtle City

Turtle City is the site that gives Gili Meno its turtle reputation. A shallow plateau on the north-east side of the island, sloping gently from 5 m to about 15 m, with sandy patches between low coral heads and seagrass beds where green and hawksbill turtles come to rest, feed, and clean.

This is where most of our students see their first turtle. On a good day you see four or five. On the best day we've ever had, our divers logged eleven different turtles in 45 minutes.

**Marine life** — green and hawksbill turtles, parrotfish, fusiliers, occasional reef sharks, nudibranchs, blue-ringed octopuses on the right rocks if you know where to look.

**Conditions** — visibility 12-20 m, current usually mild, water temperature 27-29 °C year-round.

**Best months** — May to October for the calmest water; year-round for turtles.

**Depth** — 5-15 m; ideal for new Open Water divers, snorkellers, and anyone running a shallow refresher dive.

**Photography** — wide-angle and a slow approach. Get below the turtle, not above; let it come to you.

**Etiquette** — the protected status of this site is exactly why turtles still feed here. No touching, no chasing, no flash photography on resting animals. Maintain at least one metre of distance.
