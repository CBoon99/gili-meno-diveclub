## Bio Rock

Bio Rock is a coral restoration site — a series of metal-frame structures lowered onto the sand and then run with a low-voltage current that accelerates calcium-carbonate growth on the frames. Coral fragments transplanted onto the structures grow noticeably faster than they would on natural rock, and the resulting reef is now thoroughly colonised.

It's a fascinating site for two reasons: scientifically it's a working experiment in reef restoration, and aesthetically it's beautiful — geometric metal forms slowly being eaten by life.

**Marine life** — groupers, snappers, fusilier schools, turtles cleaning themselves on the structures, nudibranchs, banded shrimp, the occasional cuttlefish.

**Conditions** — generally calm, visibility 12-18 m.

**Best months** — year-round.

**Depth** — 8-25 m.

**Suitable for** — Open Water divers (shallow side) through Advanced Adventurer (full site).

**Special features** — we run our **Eco-Scuba** course assessment dive here. Students help with reef monitoring while certifying.

**Etiquette** — do not touch the frames or the coral fragments transplanted onto them. The whole point of the site is that it's left alone.
