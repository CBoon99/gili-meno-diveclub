## Meno Wall

Meno Wall is the dramatic vertical drop on the south-west of the island. The reef edge sits at about 5 m and the wall plunges past 50 m into the channel between Meno and Gili Air. The wall is encrusted with hard and soft corals, sea fans, and barrel sponges; the blue water alongside it is alive with reef fish and the larger animals that come to feed on them.

This is the site for advanced divers wanting drama. Drop in at the top of the wall, drift along it, finish the dive on the shallow plateau among the turtles.

**Marine life** — large trevally, groupers, sweetlips, barracuda schools, turtles, white-tip and black-tip sharks, moray eels, frogfish in the sponges if you look slowly.

**Conditions** — moderate to strong current along the wall, visibility 15-25 m, the colder thermocline can drop water temperature 2-3 °C below 25 m.

**Best months** — May to October. The wet season can bring choppy surface conditions that make this site difficult to enter.

**Depth** — 5 m at the lip, 50 m+ at the toe. We dive Advanced Adventurers and Deep Specialty divers here only.

**Style** — drift dive along the wall with a safety stop at 5 m on the plateau. Morning dives have the best wall light.

**Safety** — strong currents can pin you to the wall or pull you off it. Stay close, share air at the first hint of a problem, and never chase past your turn pressure.
