## The SSI recreational ladder

Recreational diving has a clear progression. Each step builds on the last, each adds capability, and each is worth doing properly rather than rushing.

### Step 1 — Try It

**[SSI Try Scuba](/courses)** (we run this informally, ask at the shop). One pool session, one optional ocean dive. No certification, just a chance to find out if you like it.

### Step 2 — Get certified

**[SSI Open Water Diver](/courses/open-water)** — three to four days. The most popular course we run. Certifies you to dive to 18 m with a buddy, anywhere in the world.

### Step 3 — Add capability

**[SSI Advanced Adventurer](/courses/advanced-open-water)** — two days, five adventure dives. Required two are Deep and Navigation; you pick three more. Lets you dive to 30 m and is the gateway to most "interesting" dive sites worldwide.

### Step 4 — Add safety skills

**[SSI Stress & Rescue Diver](/courses/rescue-diver)** — three to four days. Often described as the course that "completes" recreational diving. Deeply changes how you dive.

### Step 5 — Specialise

**[SSI Specialty Courses](/courses/specialty-courses)** — single specialties (Deep, Night, Wreck, Drift, Photography, etc.) or grouped to earn the Specialty Diver and Master Diver ratings.

### Step 6 — Go pro

**[SSI Divemaster](/courses/divemaster)** — two-week internship. Your first professional rating. Can lead certified divers, assist instructors, work in dive shops worldwide.

### Step 7 — Teach

**SSI Open Water Instructor** — run by SSI Instructor Trainers. We can introduce you to one when you're ready.

## How long does it take?

If you arrived non-certified and dived hard:

- **Week 1** — Open Water Diver
- **Week 2** — Advanced Adventurer + a few fun dives
- **Week 3-4** — Stress & Rescue + first specialties
- **Months 2-3** — Divemaster internship

Most students don't push that hard. A common timeline is Open Water on a first holiday, Advanced on a second, then Rescue and Divemaster as a planned career change.

## Crossover from PADI / NAUI / etc

Already certified with another agency? We accept all major recreational certifications as equivalent for course prerequisites. Bring your card and your logbook and we'll work out where you slot into the SSI ladder.
