## Diving and the reef

Diving is not zero-impact. Boats use fuel. Fins kick sand. Bad buoyancy breaks coral. Tourism brings rubbish. We take the impact seriously and we'd rather over-discuss it than under-discuss it.

This page is for divers who want to know specifically what we do, what we ask of you, and how to make your visit a net positive.

## What we do

- **Small group ratios** (max 4:1) — fewer fins on the reef per instructor.
- **Mooring lines, not anchors.** All our regular sites have permanent moorings. No anchor damage.
- **No fish feeding, ever.** It's tempting and it's terrible — disrupts natural feeding behaviour, alters predator-prey balance.
- **Reef-friendly briefings.** Buoyancy, fin technique, and "no contact" are covered before every dive, not just first dives.
- **Local hires.** All our boat crew are from Gili Meno; income from diving stays on the island.
- **Trash Hero participation.** Monthly cleanup dives, plus we bag rubbish opportunistically on every dive.
- **Plastic minimisation.** Refillable water, refundable kit packaging, no individual sachets.

## What we ask of you

- **Reef-safe sunscreen only.** Oxybenzone and octinoxate kill coral. Bring zinc-based sunscreen, or buy it at our shop.
- **Practice buoyancy actively.** Even certified divers have bad days; tell your guide if you're feeling heavy and we'll fix your weight before the dive, not during.
- **Look, don't touch.** Including coral, including animals, including "just steadying yourself" on a rock that turns out to be a sponge.
- **Refill bottles at the shop.** Free, no plastic.
- **Carry your own rubbish off the boat.** Bin it on the island, not at sea.
- **Travel insurance for medical evacuations.** Saves coral indirectly: an unplanned 4-hour medevac is one of the bigger emissions events of a dive trip.

## Eco-Scuba — the course

We run an [Eco-Scuba programme](/courses/eco-scuba) for divers who want to make their entire stay a conservation contribution. Two days of theory + monitoring dives + a Trash Hero cleanup dive, ending with an SSI ecology specialty certification.

## Pairing your dives with conservation

Most of our weekly schedule has space for divers wanting to:

- **Join Trash Hero monthly cleanup days** — free for divers staying with us during the dive day
- **Help with reef monitoring at Bio Rock** — periodic, ad-hoc, ask at the shop
- **Donate equipment.** Spare diving torches, dive computers, or fins are useful — drop them at the shop and we'll route them to local Divemasters in training.

## Be honest with yourself

You don't have to do all of this to dive with us. But you do have to do the basics — reef-safe sunscreen, no touching, careful buoyancy. Those aren't optional.
